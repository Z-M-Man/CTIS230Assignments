Great Britain, the Attackers

The British Army was mainly composed of regulars--standard infantry. They are more well-known as the "Redcoats," for their distinct red uniforms.
In addition to standard soldiers, Loyalist militias and German mercenaries were also used to bolster forces.
The British campaign in the Southern States was led by Lieutenant General Charles Cornwallis.
In the weeks preceding the battle, Cornwallis had around 2,440 men. However, due to desertion and disease, he was left with around 2,100 when the battle occurred.

List of Units

Main Forces

  • 71st Regiment of Foot (Line Infantry*)
  • 23rd Regiment of Foot (Line Infantry)
  • 33rd Regiment of Foot (Line Infantry)
  • Light Infantry Company, Guards Battalion (Light Infantry**)
  • 1st Guards Battalion
  • Regiment von Bose (Hessians***)
  • Ansbach-Bayreuth Jager Company (Hessians)
  • Royal Artillery (3 six-pounders, 2 three-pounders)

Reserve Forces

  • 2nd Guards Battalion
  • Grenadier Guards Company
  • British Legion Calvary ☨


*Line infantry were standard foot soldiers organized in large groups, according to standard European strategy at the time.

**Light infantry were more mobile than line infantry units and served a screening and skirmishing role.

***Hessians were German troops hired by the British and provided additional support.

☨"Calvary" refers to both traditional calvary, who fight exclusively on horseback, and dragoons, who are infantry that use horses for transport and occaisonally fighting.
