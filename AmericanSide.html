United States of America, The Defenders

Some of the American forces were part of the Continental Army, organized by the Continental Congress to represent the new United States. A significant part of the Americans, however, were state militias and local volunteers.
The Americans at Guilford Courthouse were led by Major General Nathanael Greene.
Greene organized his forces into three lines: the first was composed of the inexperienced North Carolina militia, the second was composed of the more experienced Virginia militia, and the third was composed of trained Continentals.
In total, Greene commanded 4,100 Americans.
